Coding Club Lesson 1: Introduction to HTML

Materials Warm-up Class Activity HTML Demo Wrap-up Extra Activities

Materials Needed

  • Skill Struck platform
  • Student computing devices

Warm-up: What is an Algorithm?

Explain to students that an algorithm is like a set of instructions or steps to follow in order to complete a task. Ask them to think of examples from their daily lives or games they enjoy.

Duration: 5 minutes

Class Activity: Algorithm Dances

Choose a simple dance (e.g., "Cha Cha Slide") or create one yourself. Break down the dance into steps and explain that the sequence of steps forms an algorithm. Have students practice and then create their own dance routines with algorithms.

Example Instruction for "Cha Cha Slide":

  • Start with your feet together.
  • Take your left foot and step to the left.
  • Slide your right foot to meet your left foot.
  • Hop, etc.

Duration: 10 minutes

HTML Demo: Hello World

Ask students to share what they notice about websites and introduce HTML as the language used to build websites.

Demonstrate the basics of HTML using the Code Playground:

Hello World

Explain that <h1> is the opening tag and </h1> is the closing tag. Anything between them will appear on the website.

Duration: 10 minutes

Wrap-up

Ask students to share what challenges they faced while coding and how they solved them. Introduce the concept of "debugging" (finding and fixing mistakes).

Duration: 5 minutes

Extra Activities

  • Extend the dance activity: Have students teach their dance to the class.
  • Paper Airplane Algorithm: Write instructions for creating a paper airplane.
  • Complete the Algorithm: Give students the first and last steps of an activity and have them fill in the rest.
